# Matrix product wave function of the ground state and elementary excitations in the spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ chain


# Abstact
We propose a simple variational wave function that captures the correct ground-state energy of the spin-1 Heisenberg chain model to within 0.04%. The wave function is written in the matrix product state (MPS) form with the bond dimension $D=8$ and is characterized by three fugacity parameters. The proposed MPS generalizes the Affleck-Kennedy-Lieb-Tasaki state by dressing it with dimers, trimers, and general $q$-mers. The fugacity parameters control the number and the average size of the $q$-mers. Furthermore, the $D=8$
 variational MPS state captures the ground states of the entire family of the bilinear-biquadratic Hamiltonian belonging to the Haldane phase to high accuracy. The 2-4-2 degeneracy structure in the entanglement spectrum of our MPS state is found to match well the results of the density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) calculation, which is computationally much heavier. Spin-spin correlation functions also find an excellent fit with those obtained by DMRG.
